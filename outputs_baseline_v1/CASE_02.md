### Answer (generated by LLM based on literature)

The management of acute kidney injury (AKI) in a patient with multiple comorbidities, including renal disease, cardiovascular complications, diabetes, and metastatic malignancy, must be approached with caution. Her high BMI, prior NSAID use, and ongoing anticoagulation therapy further complicate treatment. Here are evidence-based adjustments:

1. **Discontinue Nephrotoxic Agents:**
   - Given her AKI and previous NSAID use, all nephrotoxic drugs should be discontinued if possible. NSAIDs are known to exacerbate renal impairment, and minimizing nephrotoxicity is crucial [KDOQI US commentary (pmid_23499048_p12)]. Monitoring for improvement in renal function after withdrawal is recommended.

2. **Optimize Hemodynamics and Volume Status:**
   - Ensuring optimal volume status and perfusion pressure is critical. Functional hemodynamic monitoring can help achieve this, potentially preventing worsening renal function. Careful fluid management is essential, especially since her cardiac status could be compromised. Overhydration and fluid overload must be avoided [KDOQI US commentary (pmid_23499048_p12)].

3. **Nutritional and Metabolic Management:**
   - Hyperglycemia should be avoided, with insulin therapy targeting plasma glucose levels between 110–149 mg/dl [KDOQI US commentary (pmid_23499048_p12)]. Given her severe diabetes, maintaining glucose control is crucial to prevent further complications. Additionally, assess her nutritional needs, ensuring adequate caloric intake while managing protein to prevent catabolism. Enteral nutrition is preferred if feasible [KDOQI US commentary (pmid_23499048_p12)].

If discontinuing nephrotoxic agents and optimizing hemodynamic support are not sufficient, dietary and metabolic management will be the next step. Adjustments should be made based on continuous monitoring of renal and metabolic parameters.
