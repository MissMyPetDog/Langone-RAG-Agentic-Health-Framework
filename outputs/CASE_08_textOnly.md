His current situation is complicated further by low SpO2 levels and elevated respiratory rate, indicating compromised oxygenation and potential respiratory distress. Furthermore, there is a history of exposure to nephrotoxic agents, antibiotics, and anticoagulants. Specific laboratorial markers and measurements include a low SpO2 and elevated respiratory rate. Here are the treatment options grounded in the specific patient context:

1. **Renal Replacement Therapy (RRT):**
   - **Why this patient:** Given the patient's acute kidney injury and background of congestive heart failure and severe liver disease, RRT such as dialysis may be indicated to manage fluid overload and electrolyte imbalances due to the low SpO2 and probable reduced renal perfusion. Sustained low-efficiency dialysis can cater to critically ill patients with both kidney and liver dysfunction, particularly when nephrotoxic exposure and fluid management are issues [Prospective randomized trial to assess caloric and protein needs...    - **When to use / contraindications:** RRT should be used when there is severe metabolic derangement or volume overload unresponsive to medical management. 2. **Discontinuation or Adjustment of Nephrotoxic Antibiotics:**

   - **When to use / contraindications:** Diuretics should be used sparingly in fluid overload situations where RRT is not immediately feasible.    - **Supporting sources:** [Diuretics in acute kidney injury (pmid_23499048_p130)], [Diuretics and mortality in acute renal failure (pmid_23499048_p8)].
