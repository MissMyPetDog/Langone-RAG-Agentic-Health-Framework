### Answer (generated by LLM based on literature)

Managing acute kidney injury (AKI) in an 80-year-old female with multiple comorbidities requires a careful balance of treatment priorities and risk mitigation strategies. Current evidence-based guidelines focus on discontinuing nephrotoxic agents, ensuring adequate volume status and perfusion pressure, and considering renal replacement therapy when needed.

1. **Discontinuation of Nephrotoxic Agents**: The primary recommendation is to discontinue nephrotoxic medications if possible to prevent further kidney damage. Given this patient's critical condition and exposure to nephrotoxic agents, evaluating and adjusting the medication regimen is essential. This step aligns with prioritizing changes in drug dosing and avoiding factors exacerbating kidney injury [KDOQI US commentary on the 2012 KDIGO clinical practice guideline for acute kidney injury (pmid_23499048_p12)].

2. **Maintaining Hemodynamic Stability**: Ensuring proper volume status and perfusion pressure is crucial for this patient, especially considering her low mean arterial pressure and existing cardiovascular issues. Functional hemodynamic monitoring can aid in promptly identifying and correcting hemodynamic imbalances. Strategies include administering fluids and using vasopressors, if indicated, with careful consideration of her fluid overload and congestive heart failure [KDOQI US commentary on the 2012 KDIGO clinical practice guideline for acute kidney injury (pmid_23499048_p12)].

3. **Management of Fluid Overload and Electrolyte Imbalances**: Diuretics may be considered to address fluid overload but should be used judiciously given their limited role in treating AKI. Monitoring and correcting electrolyte disturbances like hypokalemia are essential to mitigate metabolic complications. The nutritional management strategy suggests providing protein intake and keeping glucose levels within recommended targets while considering CRRT if there is inadequate response [KDOQI US commentary on the 2012 KDIGO clinical practice guideline for acute kidney injury (pmid_23499048_p12)].

These interventions should be closely coordinated with a multidisciplinary team, acknowledging potential contraindications from comorbidities and other treatment priorities. If discontinuing nephrotoxic agents is not feasible, maintaining hemodynamic stability and addressing fluid and electrolyte imbalances are prioritized.
