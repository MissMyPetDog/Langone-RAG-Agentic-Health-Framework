### Answer (generated by LLM based on literature)

In a 55-year-old male with acute kidney injury (AKI) experiencing tachycardia, low hemoglobin, and a complex history including myocardial infarction and chronic kidney disease, treatment should focus on managing the underlying causes, optimizing hemodynamics, and potentially discontinuing nephrotoxic agents. Critical values present include tachycardia, low hemoglobin, and current nephrotoxic drug usage.

1. **Discontinue Nephrotoxic Agents:**
   - **Why this patient:** The patient is on nephrotoxic agents, which can exacerbate kidney injury. Discontinuation of these when possible can help mitigate AKI progression [KDOQI US commentary (pmid_23499048_p12)].
   - **When to use / contraindications:** This approach is appropriate for patients with AKI and concurrent use of drugs known to harm renal function, such as NSAIDs, vancomycin, and certain anticoagulants. Careful transition to alternative medications is necessary to manage underlying conditions [KDOQI US commentary (pmid_23499048_p12)].
   - **Supporting sources:** [KDOQI US commentary (pmid_23499048_p12)]

2. **Ensure Volume Status and Perfusion Pressure:**
   - **Why this patient:** Given the patient's tachycardia, ensuring adequate volume status and perfusion pressure is crucial to avoid worsened kidney perfusion and further injury [KDOQI US commentary (pmid_23499048_p12)].
   - **When to use / contraindications:** This strategy is vital when signs of hemodynamic instability or reduced perfusion are present. Monitoring is necessary to avoid fluid overload, particularly in patients with heart failure [KDOQI US commentary (pmid_23499048_p12)].
   - **Supporting sources:** [KDOQI US commentary (pmid_23499048_p12)]

3. **Renal Replacement Therapy (RRT):**
   - **Why this patient:** RRT is considered when metabolic waste accumulates due to low urine output or severe electrolyte imbalances, common in patients with significant kidney impairment and low hemoglobin [KDOQI US commentary (pmid_23499048_p12)].
   - **When to use / contraindications:** Indicated in severe, refractory AKI which does not respond to conservative management. It should be considered if fluid overload, hyperkalemia, or acid-base imbalances occur [KDOQI US commentary (pmid_23499048_p12)].
   - **Supporting sources:** [KDOQI US commentary (pmid_23499048_p12)]

If discontinuation of nephrotoxic agents is not feasible due to the necessity of their therapeutic effects, ensuring appropriate volume status may still help manage the patient's hemodynamic status. If both approaches do not yield improvement, initiating RRT should be considered to prevent further deterioration.
