### Answer (generated by LLM based on literature)

In managing a 46-year-old female with acute kidney injury (AKI) (baseline creatinine 1.80 mg/dL), and comorbidities including liver disease, malignancy, and coagulopathy, it is crucial to tailor treatments carefully due to potential interactions and contraindications. Here are three evidence-based management strategies:

1. **Avoidance of Nephrotoxic Agents:**
   - **Why this patient:** Discontinuing nephrotoxic agents is critical in a patient with AKI (baseline creatinine 1.80 mg/dL) to prevent further kidney damage. Monitoring serum creatinine and urine output is recommended to assess kidney function and adjust treatment as needed [KDIGO Clinical Practice Guideline for Acute Kidney Injury (pmid_23499048_p12)].
   - **When to use / contraindications:** Avoid nephrotoxic medications, such as aminoglycosides, unless no effective alternative exists. In patients with renal impairment, the risk of further kidney damage is high [KDIGO Clinical Practice Guideline for Acute Kidney Injury (pmid_23499048_p12)]. Check for drug interactions given the complex comorbid state.
   - **Supporting sources:** [KDIGO Clinical Practice Guideline for Acute Kidney Injury (pmid_23499048_p12)].

2. **Management of Volume Overload with Diuretics:**
   - **Why this patient:** Diuretics may be used cautiously to manage volume overload in this patient with AKI, provided it does not lead to electrolyte imbalance or further kidney impairment [KDIGO Clinical Practice Guideline for Acute Kidney Injury (pmid_23499048_p12)].
   - **When to use / contraindications:** Use diuretics only in cases of significant volume overload. Avoid using them as a preventive for AKI, especially in patients with hepatic encephalopathy or risk of hemorrhage [KDIGO Clinical Practice Guideline for Acute Kidney Injury (pmid_23499048_p12)].
   - **Supporting sources:** [KDIGO Clinical Practice Guideline for Acute Kidney Injury (pmid_23499048_p12)].

3. **Selection of Antibiotics with Hepatic and Renal Sparing Profiles:**
   - **Why this patient:** Given her liver disease and renal impairment, antibiotics should be selected that have minimal hepatic and renal toxicity. Azole antifungal agents or echinocandins are recommended over conventional amphotericin B if effective for the infection, to mitigate additional organ stress [KDIGO Clinical Practice Guideline for Acute Kidney Injury (pmid_23499048_p12)].
   - **When to use / contraindications:** Avoid conventional amphotericin B due to its nephrotoxicity and consider lipid formulations instead. Monitor liver function tests and drug levels where applicable, especially considering her coagulopathy and malignancy [KDIGO Clinical Practice Guideline for Acute Kidney Injury (pmid_23499048_p12)].
   - **Supporting sources:** [KDIGO Clinical Practice Guideline for Acute Kidney Injury (pmid_23499048_p12), KDIGO Clinical Practice Guideline for Acute Kidney Injury (pmid_23499048_p8)].

These strategies should be implemented with continuous monitoring and adjustments, ensuring that the treatment accounts for her unique clinical status and any arising complications such as coagulopathy or hepatic impairment. Always collaborate with a healthcare provider to make clinical decisions.
