1. **Discontinue Diuretics:**
   - **Why this patient:** The guideline suggests not using diuretics to enhance kidney function recovery or to reduce the duration or frequency of renal replacement therapy (RRT) in AKI [KDOQI US commentary (pmid_23499048_p13)].    - **When to use / contraindications:** Discontinue in settings where AKI management is prioritized, particularly when there is no indication for diuresis to manage volume overload and where there's a risk of worsening renal perfusion [KDOQI US commentary (pmid_23499048_p13)].
   - **Supporting sources:** [KDOQI US commentary (pmid_23499048_p13)], [KDOQI US commentary (pmid_23499048_p8)].

2. **Initiate Renal Replacement Therapy (RRT):**
   - **Why this patient:** If BUN remains elevated and there are signs of fluid, electrolyte, or acid-base imbalance, initiating RRT could be essential. Such an intervention is recommended when there are life-threatening changes attributed to renal issues [KDOQI US commentary (pmid_23499048_p13)]. 
   - **When to use / contraindications:** Start RRT when intrinsic kidney function is unable to meet patient needs, especially in the ICU setting with critical comorbidities, unless contraindicated by severe bleeding risk or unstable medical conditions [KDOQI US commentary (pmid_23499048_p13), KDOQI US commentary (pmid_23499048_p10)].
   - **Supporting sources:** [KDOQI US commentary (pmid_23499048_p13)], [KDOQI US commentary (pmid_23499048_p10)].

3. **Intravenous Volume Expansion:**
   - **Why this patient:** Given the risk associated with contrast-induced AKI and potential intravascular volume depletion, i.v. volume expansion with isotonic sodium chloride or sodium bicarbonate is recommended. This approach can prevent further decline in kidney function [KDOQI US commentary (pmid_23499048_p13)].
   - **When to use / contraindications:** Suitable when patients are at increased risk for AKI, to ensure adequate organ perfusion. Should be used cautiously in heart failure to avoid fluid overload [KDOQI US commentary (pmid_23499048_p13), KDOQI US commentary (pmid_23499048_p8)].
   - **Supporting sources:** [KDOQI US commentary (pmid_23499048_p13)], [KDOQI US commentary (pmid_23499048_p8)].

If option 1 (discontinuing diuretics) is not suitable due to volume overload or CHF exacerbation, consider option 2 (initiating RRT) if renal parameters and critical illness markers worsen. If RRT is not feasible due to complications related to coagulopathy, option 3 (i.v. volume expansion) may be prioritized, carefully balancing fluid management with heart failure status.
