2. **Intravenous Volume Expansion:**
   - **Why this patient:** Due to potential risks of contrast-induced AKI from past ACEI/ARB, diuretics, NSAIDs, and vancomycin use, intravenous volume expansion with isotonic sodium chloride or bicarbonate can help mitigate AKI risk. Because the patient has diabetes and hypertension, monitoring and avoiding volume overload is critical [KDOQI US commentary (pmid_23499048_p13)], [KDOQI US commentary (pmid_23499048_p8)].
   - **When to use / contraindications:** Appropriate when risk of CI-AKI is considered, especially if imaging with contrast is unavoidable. Caution in fluid-sensitive conditions such as severe liver disease (ascites risk) [KDOQI US commentary (pmid_23499048_p13)].
   - **Supporting sources:** [KDOQI US commentary (pmid_23499048_p13)], [KDOQI US commentary (pmid_23499048_p8)].

3. **Low-dose or Iso-osmolar Contrast Media:**
   - **Why this patient:** Given the patient's high risk for contrast-induced nephropathy due to severe renal impairment, using the lowest possible dose or switching to iso-osmolar contrast media can reduce the incident risk of AKI. This is particularly relevant due to the patient's prior use of nephrotoxic medications and high baseline risk from diabetes and hypertension [KDOQI US commentary (pmid_23499048_p13)], [KDOQI US commentary (pmid_23499048_p8)].
   - **When to use / contraindications:** When imaging is necessary and cannot be substituted by non-contrast methods, ensure low adult dosage of contrast and choose iso-osmolar media. Contraindications include settings where the risk of AKI outweighs diagnostic benefits, especially in severe kidney impairment [KDOQI US commentary (pmid_23499048_p13)].
   - **Supporting sources:** [KDOQI US commentary (pmid_23499048_p13)], [KDOQI US commentary (pmid_23499048_p8)].
