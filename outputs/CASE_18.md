These suggest metabolic acidosis, anemia, and fluid overload, all of which are critical considerations in selecting appropriate treatments. 

1. **Renal Replacement Therapy (RRT):**
   - **Why this patient:** The combination of metabolic acidosis as evidenced by bicarbonate 12.0 mEq/L and fluid overload with a net positive balance of +2501 mL supports early initiation of RRT, aligning with KDIGO guidelines advocating its use to correct electrolyte imbalances and manage fluid. RRT can effectively manage significant electrolyte disturbances and fluid overload often seen in AKI [KDOQI US commentary on the 2012 KDIGO clinical practice guideline for acute kidney injury (pmid_23499048_p130)][KDOQI US commentary on the 2012 KDIGO clinical practice guideline for acute kidney injury (pmid_23499048_p3)].
   - **When to use / contraindications:** RRT is indicated in severe acidosis, electrolyte imbalances refractory to medical therapy, and significant fluid overload not responsive to diuretics. It may be less desirable in hemodynamically unstable patients unless hemodialysis options are modified (e.g., slower rates or alternative modalities).
   - **Supporting sources:** [KDOQI US commentary on the 2012 KDIGO clinical practice guideline for acute kidney injury (pmid_23499048_p130)], [KDOQI US commentary on the 2012 KDIGO clinical practice guideline for acute kidney injury (pmid_23499048_p3)].

2. **Diuretic Therapy (e.g., Furosemide):**
   - **Why this patient:** Given the net positive fluid balance, diuretic therapy may be considered to manage fluid overload if renal function allows, potentially aiding in improved renal perfusion pressure in settings of acute heart failure-related AKI [Diuretics in acute kidney injury (pmid_23499048_p130)][Diuretics and mortality in acute renal failure (pmid_23499048_p130)].
   - **When to use / contraindications:** Appropriate for use in patients who are oliguric but not anuric and have some preserved renal function. Caution is necessary with existing heart failure, as aggressive diuresis could destabilize cardiac output and worsen renal perfusion.
   - **Supporting sources:** [KDOQI US commentary on the 2012 KDIGO clinical practice guideline for acute kidney injury (pmid_23499048_p130)], [Diuretics and mortality in acute renal failure (pmid_23499048_p130)].

3. **Correcting Metabolic Acidosis:**
   - **Why this patient:** The significant acidosis indicated by bicarbonate 12.0 mEq/L warrants correction to optimize enzymatic processes and cellular function. Buffering agents or dialysis may be employed to address persistent acidosis [KDOQI US commentary on the 2012 KDIGO clinical practice guideline for acute kidney injury (pmid_23499048_p130)][KDOQI US commentary on the 2012 KDIGO clinical practice guideline for acute kidney injury (pmid_23499048_p3)].
   - **When to use / contraindications:** Indicated when acidosis is severe and symptomatic. Caution in cases where sodium intake must be limited, as buffers may contribute to sodium load.
   - **Supporting sources:** [KDOQI US commentary on the 2012 KDIGO clinical practice guideline for acute kidney injury (pmid_23499048_p130)], [KDOQI US commentary on the 2012 KDIGO clinical practice guideline for acute kidney injury (pmid_23499048_p3)].
