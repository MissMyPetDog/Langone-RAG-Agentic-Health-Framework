1. **Cholinesterase Inhibitors (e.g., Donepezil, Rivastigmine, Galantamine)**: Cholinesterase inhibitors are often utilized for mild to moderate Alzheimer's disease and other forms of dementia that display cholinergic deficits, as they work by increasing levels of acetylcholine in the brain. However, these medications should be used cautiously in patients with bradycardia or cardiac conductive abnormalities, as they can exacerbate these conditions [Structure-based classification of tauopathies (pmid_34588692_p5)].
