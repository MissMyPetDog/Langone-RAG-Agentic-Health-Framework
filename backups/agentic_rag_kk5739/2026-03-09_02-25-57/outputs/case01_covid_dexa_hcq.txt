**Problem Summary:**
The question concerns the use of dexamethasone and hydroxychloroquine in the treatment of hospitalized COVID-19 patients. The literature provided evaluates the efficacy and safety of these drugs separately in the COVID-19 context.                                                                                  

**Evidence and Trade-offs:**
1. **Dexamethasone** has been shown to decrease mortality among hospitalized COVID-19 patients, particularly those requiring invasive mechanical ventilation [Dexamethasone in Hospitalized Patients with Covid-19 (pmid_32678530_p50)]. This makes dexamethasone a compelling treatment option for severe cases, improving outcomes where respiratory support is necessary.                                                    
   
2. **Hydroxychloroquine**, either alone or in combination with azithromycin, was not shown to improve clinical status at 15 days in hospitalized COVID-19 patients with mild to moderate disease. Safety concerns include the prolongation of the corrected QT interval and elevated liver enzyme levels, which were more frequent in those receiving hydroxychloroquine [Effect of Hydroxychloroquine in Hospitalized Patients with Covid-19 (pmid_33031652_p9), (pmid_33031652_p10)].                                                 

**Treatment Suggestions:**

1. **Dexamethasone**: This is appropriate for patients hospitalized with severe COVID-19, especially those requiring mechanical ventilation. It has been shown to decrease mortality in such patients. Caution should be exercised in those with contraindications to corticosteroids, such as uncontrolled infections or significant diabetes, without close monitoring [Dexamethasone in Hospitalized Patients with Covid-19 (pmid_32678530_p50)].                                                                                    

2. **Standard Care Only**: In cases of mild to moderate COVID-19 without significant respiratory compromise or where dexamethasone is contraindicated, standard care without hydroxychloroquine or other added medications would be appropriate. This avoids the unnecessary risks associated with hydroxychloroquine [Effect of Hydroxychloroquine in Hospitalized Patients with Covid-19 (pmid_33031652_p9)].                 

3. **Supportive Care with Close Monitoring for Adverse Effects**: Should the patient have contraindications to dexamethasone or fail to meet the criteria for its use due to mild disease, focus on symptomatic and supportive care while monitoring for progression is advised. This approach minimizes the adverse effects seen with hydroxychloroquine use [Effect of Hydroxychloroquine in Hospitalized Patients with Covid-19 (pmid_33031652_p9), (pmid_33031652_p10)].
